Ideation and Wireframing

Blackboard Learn has been an essential part in student's learning. This platform helps connect students and professors by providing an easier way of teaching online. Students have the ability to learn course content, submit assignments, communicate with their fellow peers and have class through Blackboard Collaborate right in the comfort of their own homes. The one major hinderence discussed by students is their ongoing struggle of staying focused while having such a luxury to learn independently online. There was a discussion amongst a few professors at Humber College stating that many students do not read the course content that has been provided. Resulting in lower grade averages for tests and exams.

I was fortunate enough to be able to work on the pilot launch for engineering students. Where we are transforming Blackboard Learn which primarily consists of content to strictly video based. Since there was a strict deadline on the project I went directly into hi-fi wireframes and used Illustrator. We wanted the layout to be very clear and easy for students to follow. Working alongside my supervisor and the course professor it was evident that by providing only videos it would not increase the student's knowledge. That's why after each brief lecture the student would have to answer a couple questions regarding what he/she has learned: which will then help the student for final exams.

Audience and Target

We are in a world where students would prefer to learn through videos and images. Watching informational videos allows students to stay engaged with what they are learning and apply it to their studies. Our main target are students who struggle to read for long amounts of time: resulting in lower averages.

Brand Standards

When designing the final mockup I wanted to stay true to Humber College's clear brand standards. In the first iteration I decided to choose a couple different colour palettes; however, after meeting with my supervisor to discuss we decided on keeping it to one colour scheme. The two typefaces are: ITC Franklin Gothic condensed standard font and Arial for an alternative font for body copy only.
